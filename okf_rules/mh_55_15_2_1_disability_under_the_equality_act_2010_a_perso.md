# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 55

2.1 Disability 
Under the Equality Act 2010 a person is considered disabled if 
they have a physical or mental impairment that has a ‘substan-
tial’ and ‘long-term’ negative effect on their ability to do normal 
daily activities. It is estimated that approximately 21 per cent of 
the UK population (14.1 million people) are disabled, according to 
the Department for Work and Pension’s Family resources survey 
2018/2019. 
While the spatial requirements of people with mobility impair-
ments, including wheelchair users, are important in terms of 
designing the physical environment, it is necessary to understand 
the barriers experienced by people with a broad range of access 
requirements, which could be sensory, cognitive, physical or relate 
to neurodiversity. 
In general, designing to provide access, whether physical or 
intellectual, will yield results that benefit the community at large.
