# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 73

• Effects of discounting – the costs of all future work are dis-
counted to comparison on a like-for-like basis. This has the 
effect of reducing the significance and cost of future expend-
iture. The higher the discount rate, the lower the value of 
future expenditure. On this basis, high discount rates favour 
projects with lower initial capital costs. The effects of the 
discount factor can be seen in the declining cost of repair 
and maintenance over the study period. Even by year 5, at 
a discount rate of 8 per cent, the present day cost of £300 is 
discounted to £204.
