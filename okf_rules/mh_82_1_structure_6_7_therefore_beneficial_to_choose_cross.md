# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 82

Structure 6-7 
therefore beneficial to choose cross-section shapes that have large 
I-values for the given area of material. For this reason, the most 
common shape of cross-section for a steel beam is an I; a greater 
distance between the flanges results in a reduction of material for 
the required second moment of area. 
There is a limit to this, however. Since the top flange is in com-
pression, if it becomes too slender it can buckle. This is particularly 
significant in the design of steelwork and is the reason that beams 
have to be checked for lateral torsional buckling when the com-
pression flange is not fully restrained.
