# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 61

4.2 Inclusive neighbourhoods 
Inclusive neighbourhoods help to build cohesive, successful and 
sustainable communities. 
There is no defined content of an inclusive neighbourhood, but 
it should be recognised that access to good quality accessible and 
inclusive social infrastructure is vital. Social infrastructure covers a 
wide range of facilities such as health provision, educational, com-
munity, cultural, play, recreation and sports facilities, and green 
infrastructure. Inclusion in the widest sense should be married with 
environmental, economic and social sustainability. As well as look-
ing at physical factors, this should also address health impacts that 
create unintentional barriers for under-represented groups. 
One underlying concept that supports inclusive neighbourhoods 
is – like that of lifetime homes – making neighbourhoods adaptable 
and addressing peoples’ differing needs over the different stages of 
their lives. An inclusive neighbourhood should be welcoming, easy 
to move around, enjoyable and somewhere they feel secure. There 
should be a focus on the inclusivity of connections and spaces 
between buildings, which are often overlooked.
