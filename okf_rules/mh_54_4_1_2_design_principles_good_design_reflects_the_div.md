# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 54

1.2 Design principles 
Good design reflects the diverse nature of people and does not 
impose barriers of any kind, whether physical, cultural or social. 
The design of the built environment can form barriers that are, or 
can become, disabling to all people. Designing inclusively guar-
antees accessibility to all and considers age, social circumstance, 
gender, disability, sexual orientation and faith. 
The British Standards Institution’s (BSI) BS 7000–6:2005 
Design management systems – Part 6: Managing inclusive design 
identified that inclusive design recognises diversity by addressing 
the ability and preferences of people, covering elements such as 
age, disability, culture, gender, allergies and cognitive abilities. 
Making environments easy to use for everyone requires consider-
ation other than just physical factors – it is also about experience. 
How people feel when using a building or space is of equal impor-
tance to their physical needs.
