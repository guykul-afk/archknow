# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 63

4.7 Inclusive cycling 
An inclusive approach to cycling should accommodate a wide 
range of cycle types and not exclude or disadvantage their riders. 
This includes people with physical, sensory and cognitive impair-
ments who use handcycles, tricycles (both upright and recumbent), 
tandems, solo bikes with or without adaptations to suit the rider’s 
specific needs (e.g. one-handed brakes, crutch holders and power 
assisted) and cargo bikes and bikes with trailers used by parents 
with children. 
Cycle infrastructure, such as cycle parking, should accommo-
date all-ability cycles with cycle parking within buildings accessed 
by step-free routes. Transport for London recommends that at least 
5 per cent of parking spaces should cater for non-standard cycles 
(using Sheffield style stands rather than stackers) and that if lift 
access is provided to cycle stores then the lift should have a min-
imum car size of 1.2 m wide by 2.3 m deep with a 900 mm door 
opening.
