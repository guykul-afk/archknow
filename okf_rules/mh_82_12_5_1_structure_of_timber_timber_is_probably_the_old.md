# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 82

5.1 Structure of timber 
Timber is probably the oldest building material used. Wood is com-
posed of hollow tubular fibres of cellulose impregnated with the 
resin lignin, packed closely together not unlike a bundle of drinking 
straws. The result is that the material is strong in the longitudinal 
direction – in tension and compression – but weak along the inter-
face between the fibres.
