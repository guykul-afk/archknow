# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 55

1.7 User engagement 
User consultation and the involvement of local people, including local 
disabled people’s groups, also help to highlight particular, sometimes 
local, access issues that need to be addressed. People are the experts in 
their own lived experience and the barriers they face. Giving excluded 
people the opportunity to bring their personal experiences into the 
design process through co-design enriches design development. This 
was the case with the design of the climbing experience Up at the O2 
in Greenwich, London (Figure 4.1), which has been made accessible 
to a wide range of disabled people including wheelchair users.
