# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 64

5.5 Gender-neutral toilets 
There is no UK legislation that enshrines in law that non gendered 
toilets are to be provided. However, on private premises the man-
ager or licensee has the right to determine entry to the premises and 
the right to decide who uses which toilet and any other gendered 
facilities such as changing rooms etc. 
Emerging good practice is to let people use the facilities appro-
priate to the way they present and as those who have the protected 
characteristic of gender reassignment should not be discriminated 
against purely because they are transsexual. A proportionate solu-
tion should be found. The Equality Act 2010 defines a person as 
having the protected characteristic of gender reassignment (trans-
sexualism) if the person is proposing to undergo, is undergoing or 
has undergone a process (or part of a process) for the purpose of 
reassigning the person’s sex by changing physiological or other 
attributes of sex. 
Consideration should be given to providing gender-neutral 
toilets that people of any gender or gender identity may use. 
Gender-neutral toilets should preferably be self-contained. Not 
all toilets should be defined as gender-neutral, as this may be 
problematic for certain faith groups who require separate sex 
facilities. 
Wheelchair accessible toilets are unisex; there is nothing that 
precludes them as being assigned as gender-neutral. However, this 
is not recommended in practice as this could make the accessible 
toilets less available to the disabled people that need them.
