# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 64

4.9 Emergency egress 
Evacuation for disabled people is a combination of physical and 
management procedures plus a liaison between the building man-
agers and occupants. The evacuation strategy for disabled people 
must include those with sensory and cognitive disabilities and 
should be developed in conjunction with the fire strategy and 
management policies, with reference made to good practice rec-
ommendations within the BS 9999:2017 Code of practice for fire 
safety in the design, management and use of buildings. 
All building users should be able to evacuate from a building 
with dignity and by as independent means as possible. Emergency 
carry up/down devices that rely on manual handling are not con-
sidered to be appropriate for reasons of dignity and independence. 
The installation of lifts which can be used for evacuation purposes 
provide a dignified and more independent solution and are there-
fore recommended. 
BS 9999 indicates that the preferred method of evacuation for 
disabled people is by horizontal evacuation to the outside of the 
building, or another fire compartment, or by evacuation lift. If these 
are not available or not in operation, then it might be necessary to 
implement ‘carry up’ or ‘carry down’. This approach has been cod-
ified in the London Plan Policies Inclusive Design and Fire Safety. 
These require that in all developments where lifts are installed, as 
a minimum at least one lift per core (or more subject to capacity 
assessments) should be a suitably sized fire evacuation lift suitable 
to be used to evacuate people who require level access from the 
building.
