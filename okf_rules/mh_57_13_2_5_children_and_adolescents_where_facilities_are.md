# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 57

2.5 Children and adolescents 
Where facilities are to be used solely by small children, specific 
heights should be adjusted to meet their requirements. There are 
approximately four million children aged five or under. Therefore, 
design in general should also consider the needs of younger chil-
dren, for example, when providing family facilities or sinks at 
lower heights in toilets. There is limited guidance on the design of 
the built environment for disabled children, and consultation with 
stakeholders can provide invaluable information based on practice 
and precedent. 
Disabled children and young people currently face multiple bar-
riers, making it more difficult for them to achieve their potential, to 
achieve the outcomes their peers expect and to succeed in education. 
Disabled parents and carers of children should also be consid-
ered at all stages of the design process. Where family facilities 
are provided, for example baby changing rooms, these should be 
accessible to both disabled and non-disabled parents/carers.
