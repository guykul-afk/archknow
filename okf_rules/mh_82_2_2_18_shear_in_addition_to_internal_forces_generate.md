# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 82

2.18 Shear 
In addition to internal forces generated by bending moments, most 
beam cross-sections will also have to carry a force in the plane of 
the section, called a shear force. Generally, the shear will be great-
est at the supports of a beam, and least at mid-span. 
In the case of an I section, the shear force acts mainly within the 
web connecting together the two flanges that are in compression 
and tension. If the web becomes too slender, it can buckle under the 
influence of the shear force.
