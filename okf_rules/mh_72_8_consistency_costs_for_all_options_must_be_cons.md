# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 72

• Consistency – costs for all options must be consistently cal-
culated. Where appropriate, both cost and income streams 
associated with each option should be considered. Where one 
option can deliver an enhanced benefit (for example a reduc-
tion in energy usage) this can be included in the assessment to 
offset the cost of maintenance and disposal. For example, all 
cleaning and maintenance costs should be included in a floor 
finishes assessment, together with energy reduction or opera-
tional maintenance benefits, as well as any revenues associated 
with the disposal of high value, long-life assets such as natural 
finishes.
