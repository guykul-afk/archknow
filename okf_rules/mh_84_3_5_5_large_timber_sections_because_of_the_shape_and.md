5.5 Large timber sections 
Because of the shape and composition of the tree trunks from 
which timber comes, it is difficult to directly produce the larger size 
sections often required for modern construction, particularly for 
beams. For such larger sections, smaller timbers are glued together 
to form laminated beams, Figure 6.5, or load-bearing units of cross

purposes, and manufacturers produce handbooks giving compre-

and CLT are often referred to as ‘engineered timber’ due to their 
increased stability and more predictable structural characteristics.
