# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 55

1.6 RIBA Plan of Work 
The RIBA Plan of Work provides a framework for architects to 
use with their clients, bringing greater clarity to the different stages 
of a project. The National Register of Access Consultants set out 
a process for delivering inclusive environments to align with the 
2013 version of the Plan of Work. Table I highlights and explains 
what activity should be undertaken at each RIBA work stage to 
embed the principles of inclusive design from project inception to 
completion. It is important that the inclusive design process should 
be documented, reviewed and monitored throughout the different 
stages of the project. Though now superseded, this is still a useful 
reference to the inclusive design inputs at each stage. 
In 2020, RIBA revised the Plan of Work to address changes in 
the industry such as digital innovation, modern methods of con-
struction, and changes in project drivers, including delivering best 
value and an increased awareness of ethics. For the first time, the 
Plan of Work now incorporates an inclusive design strategy. RIBA 
acknowledges that accessibility and inclusion for disabled people 
is a firm policy of Government and the aspiration and expectation 
of the general public. The Plan of Work identifies that the inclu-
sive design strategy should consider all of these and be developed 
at the outset and implemented over the building’s useful life. The 
codes of practice that form the two parts of BS 8300:2018 provide 
a detailed breakdown of the contents of an inclusive design strategy 
for each stage of a project.
