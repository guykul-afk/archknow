# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 86

6.5 Mesh reinforcement 
For many structural elements, such as slabs and walls, it is conven-
ient to use reinforcement in the form of a pre-welded mesh consisting 
of bars in both directions. Care should be taken in specifying the 
correct mesh as they have different areas of bars in each direction 
depending upon their designation letter. An ‘A’ square mesh has the 
same area of bars in each direction. A ‘B’ structural mesh has larger 
bars in the main direction and the area of bars in the cross direction 
satisfies the minimum area of secondary reinforcement requirement 
of BS EN 1992–1–1: 2004 + A1: 2014. It is important therefore to 
ensure that a structural mesh is oriented correctly, as indicated on the 
reinforcement drawing, in order that structural strength is not com-
promised. A ‘C’ long mesh is similar to a ‘B’ mesh except that the 
area of cross wires does not meet the requirements of the standard. 
These meshes are generally used for reinforcing slabs on ground.
