# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 57

2.6 Large people 
Obesity is more common among people from areas of higher depri-
vation, older age groups, some ethnic minority groups and disabled 
people. The Health Survey for England 2019 found that 68 per cent of 
men and 60 per cent of women are overweight (BMI ≥ 25 kg/m2) or 
obese (BMI ≥ 30 kg/m2). Obesity prevalence rose between 1993 and 
2019 from 13 to 27 per cent for men and from 16 to 29 per cent for 
women. The survey found that one in five children in reception year 
are overweight or obese, rising to one in three children in Year 6. 
Both raised BMI and very high waist circumference are thought 
to be independent predictors of future obesity-related ill health, and 
are considered predictive measures for identifying an individual’s 
risk of obesity-related conditions including high cholesterol, high 
blood pressure, diabetes, bone and joint problems, and breathing 
difficulties – as well as premature adult mortality. 
Pregnant women, like larger people, can also be disadvantaged 
by the design of the environment. Long routes and stairs can be 
very tiring; narrow seats, doors and small toilet cubicles are other 
common barriers. 
In certain buildings such as football stadia, deliberately narrow 
doorways are used to ensure control over entry. In these cases, and 
also where turnstiles are used, additional provision for large people 
should be made.
