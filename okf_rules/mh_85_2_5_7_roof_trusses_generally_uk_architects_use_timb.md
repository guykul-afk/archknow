# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 85

5.7 Roof trusses 
Generally, UK architects use timber in two common places: 
roof trusses and floor joists. Nowadays, most trusses are of the 
gang-nail type supplied to order for the required conditions. The 
manufacturer will supply calculations based on BS EN 1995–1–1: 
2004 + A2: 2014 Eurocode 5 for submission to the local building 
inspector. PD 6693–1: 2019 gives guidance on maximum roof 
truss spans for single-, two- and three-storey, mono- and duo-
pitch buildings, according to their pitch and wind zone locations 
within the UK.
