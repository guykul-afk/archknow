6 CONCLUSION 
Clients will judge the success of their capital projects by many criteria, 
and there is no doubt that the imagination shown by the design team 
in delivering a carefully targeted solution to their client’s require-
ments, together with a commitment to achieving a good level of 
finished quality, will be very high on most clients’ agendas. Delivery 
on budget is also usually a high priority, and unlike other aspects of 
project delivery, this is easy to monitor and measure. Design teams 
need to work closely with their cost consultants to make sure that 
budgets are appropriate, and that design solutions directly address the 
aspects of the client’s brief which deliver greatest value. 
Techniques such as whole life costing and value management can 
be used by the cost consultant and wider design/project team to help 
to identify design solutions which best meet the client’s requirements. 
By collaborating closely from the earlier stages of a project to 
establish an appropriate capital cost budget, and by maintaining 
the discipline of working within the budget set by the client and 
project team, the design team will give itself the best opportunity 
of delivering a project which meets the design team’s expectations, 
the client’s requirements and provides all parties with an appropri-
ate financial reward.
