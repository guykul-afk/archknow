# Accessibility Standard: Metric Handbook Accessibility

## Details from Metric Handbook, Page 63

4.8 Faith and cultural provision 
Building Regulations do not provide design guidance or consid-
erations for the different faiths and cultures of building users, and 
there is limited guidance within British Standards. As discussed 
previously, although the Equality Act covers faith, it does not 
specifically require the design of the built environment to con-
sider faith. 
From an inclusive design perspective, it is important to consider 
this as part of the analysis and response to user need. Depending 
on the nature of the building, the local population and the build-
ing’s users will dictate how the design should accommodate 
differing faiths. Large-scale shopping facilities, transport hubs, 
civic buildings and educational establishments are likely to have 
large culturally diverse populations and, as such, are likely to have 
a defined need for such facilities. In developing the building brief 
designers should be cognisant of the need to address faith and cul-
ture within the overall spatial requirements of the building; see 
Figure 4.12. 
Chapter 31 identifies key considerations for places of worship 
and identifies design features, notably for sanitary facilities, that 
should be considered for certain faiths. Where multi-faith rooms 
are provided, they should be provided with adjacent wash (ablu-
tion) facilities and storage facilities.
